Reserch Interests

My research focuses on computational materials science, particularly the design and optimization of radiation-resistant materials through multi-scale modeling approaches. My work integrates molecular dynamics simulations, density functional theory (DFT) calculations, and machine learning techniques to address fundamental challenges in nuclear materials.

  1. Radiation Effects in Advanced Materials
    1.1 Development of novel strategies for enhancing radiation tolerance through structural engineering and interface design
    1.2 Development of novel strategies for enhancing radiation tolerance through chemical composition design
    1.3 Investigation of irradiation response of materials under external stimuli
  2. Multi-scale modeling of mechanical properties and failure mechanisms
  3. Development of interatomic potentials for complex material systems

    3.2 Machine learning potential

3. Development of interatomic potentials for complex material systems

3.1 2NN-MEAM potential

This potential is designed for simulations focused on understanding and predicting the behavior of irradiation resistance of Ni-Al-Co-Ti system, emphasizing their structural order. The parameters have been refined to accurately reflect the site preference of Ni, Co, Al, and Ti elements in the L12 A3B intermetallic structure. The potential effectively reproduces key physical properties such as lattice constants, defect formation energies, and defect kinetics, demonstrating reasonable agreement with experimental data and first-principles calculations. Furthermore, this potential was used to systematically explore the irradiation resistance and kinetic reordering capabilities of the multicomponent intermetallic systems, showing good consistency with experimental observations.

The potential files are also shared on:
https://www.ctcms.nist.gov/potentials/system/Ni/#Al-Co-Ni-Ti

Or in my github:
https://github.com/shashahuangss/2NN-MEAM-potential/tree/main/MEAM-potential

3.2 Machine learning potential

This machine-learned potential is developed by deepmd-kit to explores the role of off-stoichiometry on the mechanical properties and grain boundary (GB) strength of ZrC. The training dataset includes perfect crystal, defect-containing cells (including single C vacancy, single Zr vacancy, single C interstitial, single Zr interstitial, and carbon di-vacancy composed of two nearest C atoms C-C), as well as various surface structures including (100), (110), (111), (210), (112), (221), and (310) surfaces.
For each system, ab initio molecular dynamics (AIMD) simulations were performed in the NVT ensemble using the Nose-Hoover thermostat at 300 K, 600 K, 900 K, 1200 K, 1500 K, and 2000 K. Additionally, AIMD simulations were conducted for both perfect and defect-containing models at 300 K and 1500 K, with uniform expansion and contraction of the cell along all axes by a scaling factor ranging from 0.96 to 1.04, in an increment of 0.02. For surface models, strain was applied along the two axes parallel to the surface plane.

The potential file is shared on: https://github.com/shashahuangss/Machine-learning-potential
